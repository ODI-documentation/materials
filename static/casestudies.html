Case studies

The three case studies concern, as far as the first two are concerned, the first and second stories of the first six titled (Storia dell'ingrato punito and Storia dell'alchimista che vendette l'anima); the last case study concerns some cards of the sixth, and last, story enclosed within the last chapter of the collection, Tutte le altre storie.

Case study 1: some cards from Storia dell'ingrato punito

After introducing himself, the protagonist (Cavaliere di Coppe) arranges 17 tarot cards gradually. In summary, the narrative depicts a knight who is robbed and hanged from a tree (Il Penduto) branch by a brigand (La Forza) but subsequently rescued by a maiden (La Temperanza). The said maiden, in turn, resolves to avenge the knight when he forsakes her to wed a woman of greater affluence (L'Imperatrice). In the denouement of the story, the knight meets his demise (Otto di Spade) at the hands of Cybele (La Papessa) and her adherents.

Therefore, the Cavaliere di Coppe (odi:StoryCard) is the protagonist of Storia dell'ingrato punito (odi:Story). In BACODI the card is represented in relation to the tarot deck (odi:TarotDeck) and the story (odi:Story). In the first case, it is an artefact belonging to the tarot deck (odi:TarotCard) with its main characteristics (e.g. the Cavaliere di Coppeis a "court card" (odi:Typology) and its suit is "cups" (odi:hasSuit, odi:Suit). In the second case, it is a narrative istance (odi:StoryCard) and it represents a character (odi:Character) with a specific meaning (the protagonist, odi:Meaning).

The Storia dell'ingrato punito is an example of how two cards can be used to highlight an evolution of the same representation: the passage of the protagonist from a wealthy condition to a condition of poverty is expressed by a change (odi:changesIn) of cards: from Cavaliere di Coppe to Il Penduto.

In BACODI this evolution of the character is expressed as below.


          bacodi:cavaliere-di-coppe-storiaUno-protagonista a odi:Character ;
          odi:changesIn bacodi:il-penduto-storiaUno-protagonista .

Case study 2: some cards from Storia dell'alchimista che vendette l'anima

The second protagonist fits perpendicularly to the first story and takes the Asso di Coppe and La Papessa as its point of agreement. The story tells of an alchemist (Re di Coppe) who, during his search for the source of life, encounters a witch (La Papessa) who predicts his future. The alchemist comes across a visitor (Il Bagatto) who turns out to be the devil (Il Diavolo). He promises the alchemist a city made of gold in exchange for the souls of all the city's inhabitants. The alchemist agrees and gears are set in motion (La Ruota della Fortuna) for the transformation into gold of the whole city (Nove di Coppe, Sei di Denari). Armed guards bar the entrance to anyone who wants to enter, including a nymph (La Temperanza). Even in this case, the Re di Coppe (odi:StoryCard) is the protagonist (odi:Meaning) and it has got several same characteristics as the Cavaliere di Coppe of the previous case study, in relation to both the tarot deck (odi:TarotDeck) and the story (odi:Story). In this case, is shown the relationships between the Re di Coppe and Il Bagatto (odi:bumpsInto) as an example of narrative relations between a the protagonist card and its cards and the identity of representation between Il Bagatto and Il Diavolo (odi:sameAs).

In BACODI both the narrative relation and the identity of representation are expressed as below.


        bacodi:re-di-coppe-storiaDue-protagonista a odi:Character ;
        odi:bumpsInto bacodi:il-bagatto-storiaDue-protagonista .
        

        bacodi:il-bagatto-storiaDue-visitatore a odi:Character ;
        odi:hasMeaningOf bacodi:visitatore ;
        odi:sameAs bacodi:il-diavolo-storiaDue-visitatore .

Not all cards have a direct relation to the protagonist card: relations can be "simple", the direct ones, or "complex"(odi:hasComplexRelationWith). As far "complex relationships" are concerned, cards that have a direct relationship can either be specified (odi:isSpecifiedBy) or have a general relationship (odi:isRelatedWith) with the card that has an indirect relationship with the protagonist card. In Storia dell'alchimista che vendette l'anima the moment of the cityâ€™s transformation into gold is a clear example of how cards can have a "complex relationships" with the protagonist card. The event involves three cards: La Ruota della Fortuna, the Nove di Coppe and the Sei di Denari. While La Ruota della Fortuna has a direct relationship with the main character (odi:carriedOutBy), the same cannot be said for the Nove di Coppe and the Sei di Denari. In this case, then, these two numeral cards are used to specify (odi:isSpecifiedWith) the event represented by the triumph.

In BACODI, the "simple relationships" between the protagonist and the event is expressed as below.


      bacodi:ruota-della-fortuna-storiaDue- la_trasformazione_di_tutto_in_oro a odi:Event ;
      odi:carriedOutBy bacodi:re-di-coppe-storiaDue-protagonista .

Otherwise, the "complex relationships" between the Nove di Coppe and the protagonist are expressed as below. 


      bacodi:re-di-coppe-storiaDue-protagonista a odi:Character ;
      odi:hasComplexRelationWith
      bacodi:nove-di-coppe-storiaDue-ricchezza.

Case study 3: some cards from the last story of Tutte le altre storie

The story tells of a boarder (Regina di Bastoni) who challenges a warlord (Cavaliere di Spade) to a duel. She becomes a swordswoman (La Giustizia) and the warlord falls in love with her. The wedding is celebrated. As soon as the groom turns away, the in-laws decide to pay a banjo to kill the bride. The scherano rushes at the bride (La Forza), but the latter finds himself tied upside down, defeated by the woman. In the guise of a hostess (La Temperanza), the protagonist awaits the return of the groom (La Temperanza) and sets a table for two.

Even in this case, the court card (odi:StoryCard) is the protagonist (odi:Meaning) of the story (odi:Story) and, in BACODI, is represented in relation to both the tarot deck (odi:TarotDeck)and the story.

It is noteworthy that there are other two cards representing the protagonist: La Giustizia and La Temperanza. In the former there is a change of character (odi:changesIn), while in the latter an identity (odi:sameAs).
