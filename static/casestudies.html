Case studies

Here three case studies are shown. The first two concern the first and second stories of the first six titled (Storia dell'ingrato punito and Storia dell'alchimista che vendette l'anima); the last case study concerns some cards of the sixth, and last, story enclosed within the last chapter of the collection, Tutte le altre storie.

Case study 1: some cards from Storia dell'ingrato punito

The protagonist of Storia dell'ingrato punito (Cavaliere di Coppe) arranges 17 tarot cards gradually. Briefly, the story tells about a knight who is robbed and hanged from a tree (Il Penduto) branch by a brigand (La Forza) but subsequently rescued by a maiden (La Temperanza). The said maiden, in turn, resolves to avenge the knight when he forsakes her to wed a woman of greater affluence (L'Imperatrice). Finally, the knight meets his demise (Otto di Spade) at the hands of Cybele (La Papessa) and her adherents.

In BACODI the Cavaliere di Coppe (odi:StoryCard) is represented in relation to the tarot deck (odi:TarotDeck) and the story (odi:Story). In the first case, it is an artefact belonging to the tarot deck (odi:TarotCard) and thus it is described through its main characteristics (e.g. the Cavaliere di Coppeis a "court card" (odi:Typology) and its suit is "cups" (odi:hasSuit, odi:Suit)). In the second case, as narrative istance (odi:StoryCard), it represents a character (odi:Character) with a specific meaning (the protagonist, odi:Meaning).

The Storia dell'ingrato punito is an example of how two cards can be used to represent an evolution of the same representation: the changing of the protagonist from a wealthy condition to a condition of poverty is expressed by a change (odi:changesIn) of cards: from Cavaliere di Coppe to Il Penduto.

In BACODI this changing of the character is expressed as below.


          bacodi:cavaliere-di-coppe-storiaUno-protagonista a odi:Character ;
          odi:changesIn bacodi:il-penduto-storiaUno-protagonista .

Case study 2: some cards from Storia dell'alchimista che vendette l'anima

The protagonist of Storia dell'alchimista che vendette l'anima (Re di Coppe) fits perpendicularly to the first story and takes the Asso di Coppe and La Papessa as its point of agreement. The story tells about an alchemist (Re di Coppe) who, during his search for the source of life, encounters a witch (La Papessa) who predicts his future. The alchemist comes across a visitor (Il Bagatto) who turns out to be the devil (Il Diavolo). He promises the alchemist a city of gold in exchange for the souls of all the city's inhabitants. The alchemist agrees and gears are set in motion (La Ruota della Fortuna) for the transformation into gold of the whole city (Nove di Coppe, Sei di Denari). Armed guards bar the entrance to anyone who wants to enter, including a nymph (La Temperanza).

Even in this case, the Re di Coppe (odi:StoryCard) is described as an artefact of the tarot deck (odi:TarotDeck) and as the protagonist (odi:Meaning) of the story (odi:Story). In both cases, the card has got several same characteristics as the Cavaliere di Coppe of the previous case study. In this case, is shown the relationships between the Re di Coppe and Il Bagatto (odi:bumpsInto), as an example of narrative relations between the protagonist and its cards, then the semantic link between Il Bagatto and Il Diavolo (odi:sameAs) to represent the identity of representation

.

In BACODI both the narrative relation and the semantic link are expressed as below.


        bacodi:re-di-coppe-storiaDue-protagonista a odi:Character ;
        odi:bumpsInto bacodi:il-bagatto-storiaDue-protagonista .
        

        bacodi:il-bagatto-storiaDue-visitatore a odi:Character ;
        odi:hasMeaningOf bacodi:visitatore ;
        odi:sameAs bacodi:il-diavolo-storiaDue-visitatore .

In each story of the collection, cards can have "simple relations", direct relationships, or "complex relations"(odi:hasComplexRelationWith), indirect relationships. As far "complex relations" are concerned, cards that have a direct relationship with the protagonist can either be specified (odi:isSpecifiedBy) or have a general relationship (odi:isRelatedWith) with the card that has got an indirect relationship with the protagonist. In Storia dell'alchimista che vendette l'anima the moment of the cityâ€™s transformation into gold is a clear example of how two cards have a "complex relationships" with the protagonist. The event involves three cards: La Ruota della Fortuna, the Nove di Coppe and the Sei di Denari. La Ruota della Fortuna has got a direct relationship with the main character (odi:carriedOutBy), whereas the numerals Nove di Coppe and the Sei di Denari has got an indirect relationship with the protagonist and they are used to specify (odi:isSpecifiedWith) the event represented by the triumph.

In BACODI, the "simple relationships" between the protagonist and the event is expressed as below.


      bacodi:ruota-della-fortuna-storiaDue- la_trasformazione_di_tutto_in_oro a odi:Event ;
      odi:carriedOutBy bacodi:re-di-coppe-storiaDue-protagonista .

Otherwise, the "complex relationships" between the Nove di Coppe and the protagonist are expressed as below. 


      bacodi:re-di-coppe-storiaDue-protagonista a odi:Character ;
      odi:hasComplexRelationWith
      bacodi:nove-di-coppe-storiaDue-ricchezza.

Case study 3: some cards from the last story of Tutte le altre storie

This last story of the collection tells about a boarder (Regina di Bastoni) who challenges a warlord (Cavaliere di Spade) to a duel. She becomes a swordswoman (La Giustizia) and the warlord falls in love with her. The wedding is celebrated. As soon as the groom turns away, the in-laws decide to pay a banjo to kill the bride. The scherano rushes at the bride (La Forza), but the latter finds himself tied upside down, defeated by the woman. In the guise of a hostess (La Temperanza), the protagonist awaits the return of the groom (La Temperanza) and sets a table for two.

Even in this case, the Regina di Bastoni is a court card of the tarot deck (odi:TarotDeck) and it has got characteristics related to its presence in the deck. In addition, the card is a narrative istance of the story

(odi:StoryCard) as character (odi:Character) which has got the role of protagonist (odi:Meaning) of the story (odi:Story).

It is noteworthy that there are other two cards representing the protagonist: La Giustizia and La Temperanza. In the former there is a change of character (odi:changesIn), while in the latter an identity (odi:sameAs). Therefore, La Temperanza who saves the protagonist of the first story, now is the protagonist

.

In BACODI, the semantic link between the Regina di Bastoni and the two triumphs La Temperanza and La Giustizia are formal described as below

      odi:Character ;
      odi:changesIn bacodi:la-temperanza-tuttelealtrestorieSei-
      protagonista ;
      odi:sameAs bacodi:la-giustizia-tuttelealtrestorieSei-
      protagonista .
